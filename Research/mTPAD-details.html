Robotics And Rehabilitation (RoAR) Lab

What is mTPAD - mobile tethered pelvic assist device

The mTPAD (mobile Tethered Pelvic Assist Device) is a cable-driven robotic platform. It is a portable extension of the Tethered Pelvic Assist Device (TPAD). It aims to assist individuals with irregular gait by providing targeted external forces to the pelvis without restricting lower limb movement. Unlike the traditional TPAD, which relies on a treadmill setup with motion capture for precise force application, the mTPAD is designed for overground use. It eliminates the need for external motion capture systems and tension sensors, employing an open-loop controller based on forward kinematics for pelvic localization and force control.

mTPAD Control System

The mTPAD control system uses an open-loop strategy based on geometric forward kinematics to estimate the pelvis position from motor encoder angles, bypassing the need for external localization sensors. The controller calculates the necessary cable lengths and determines the pelvic center using a 2-3-2 cable configuration. It then applies a target force-moment profile by solving for the optimal cable tensions via quadratic programming. These tensions are mapped to motor currents, with each motor using an internal PID controller for precise force application. 8 Motors Optimization Problem

Motivation

The previous mTPAD design, with 7 motors, could not provide torque around the vertical axis and had limited capacity to achieve desired force and moment outputs. Additionally, unresolved issues led to a discontinuous wrench profile. To address these limitations, we planned to add an 8th motor, enhancing performance and enabling smoother, more continuous force application.

Approach

Define a feasible region around the mTPAD frame for potential motor placements. Explore this region to identify motor positions that can generate the desired forces and moments without interfering with the frame structure. Use the volume of the convex hull to represent the workspace of the end-effector. Finally, choose the configuration that maximizes the workspace volume.
