Robot Eye

Robotics Hackathon 2024

This was a 24-hour hardware hackathon organized by the Columbia University Robotics Club. With no strict guidelines or specific project requirements, participants were free to build anything they imagined. My teammates and I built a robot eye that can track human movement——yes, it's "always watching you!"

Mechanical Design

The eyeball was a camera. It had two degrees of freedom and was connected with two ball joints and one universal joint. The upper and lower eyelids were simultaneously controlled by a single servo.

Tracking Module

We utilized MediaPipe with OpenCV to detect human faces and extract the coordinates of the closest one. By adjusting the camera's position to keep the face centered in the frame, we achieved real-time human face tracking.

LED Module

We attached an LED to the eyelid. It could detect the musical beats from any piece of music and flashed with the rhythm.

We got Third Prize!
